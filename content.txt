# HANTAMASK

## 1. Project Lore

In 2019, an unnamed Zurich-based cryptography studio began developing a wallet protocol for "high-risk asset containment." The project was shelved after regulators questioned why the interface resembled biosafety cabinet controls. Three years later, the source code surfaced on an obscure pastebin — annotated entirely in haiku.

HANTAMASK emerged from that leak. The biohazard trefoil was never meant to be a mascot; it was a warning label on the original hardware prototype's casing. Early adopters kept the symbol as an inside joke. The joke became a brand. The brand became a philosophy: treat your portfolio like a pathogen — isolate it, contain it, let nothing escape without authorization.

Today, HANTAMASK operates on a single principle borrowed from Level 4 laboratories: redundancy is not paranoia, it is protocol. Every transaction passes through airlocked confirmation. Every seed phrase is treated as a controlled substance. The wallet doesn't ask if you're sure. It assumes you're never sure enough.
